# Design notes

The interface behaves like a working studio, not a dashboard in evening wear.

Its visual grammar is cool uncoated paper, graphite, one pink thread, one dry cobalt interruption, translucent color, exact typography and plenty of air. The hero artwork stays abstract enough to be felt before it is decoded. Film grain is local, quiet and disposable when reduced-transparency preferences ask for less.

Motion has a job: reveal depth, preserve place, make a correction feel reversible, or let color behave like material. Reduced-motion users receive the same hierarchy without the performance.

The deeper rule is more important than the palette: complexity appears at the moment it becomes useful. Evidence is never hidden; it simply does not stand in the doorway.
