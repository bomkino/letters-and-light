# Product guide

Letters & Light solves two neighboring problems without pretending they are one problem.

Type decides how the deck speaks and how comfortably it reads. Color decides how the deck holds light, emphasis, data and contrast. A person may need either half or both. The home page makes that choice explicit; the shared direction appears only after both pieces exist.

## Type route

1. Choose a short or deep brief.
2. Answer only the questions required by that route.
3. Receive five editorially distinct directions rendered in their exact font files.
4. Shuffle the set, star families, lock a role, remove a bad fit, or undo the last move.
5. Test bounded headline and paragraph copy on real slide jobs.
6. Export sources, settings and evidence.

The recommendation is not a popularity list. It filters on practical eligibility, then builds lanes with different editorial jobs: reliable, characterful, editorial, wildcard and one-family.

## Color route

1. Drop, paste or choose one image.
2. Take the quick route or add a guided brief.
3. See the four essential roles before the complete system.
4. Move between light and dark companions, shuffle viable systems, star favorites and lock roles.
5. Correct individual colors with reversible operations.
6. Inspect the source map, contrast status and complete role table only when useful.

The original image never leaves the browser. Extraction keeps spatial evidence and source proportions; the recommendation assigns jobs rather than merely printing dominant swatches.

## Full look

The full route composes a selected type direction and selected color system. Composition may explain the relationship and render a shared slide. It may not change engine evidence, upgrade a candidate, hide a contrast failure or invent application support.

## Result philosophy

The tool returns a direction, not divine law. Every result should answer:

- What should I use?
- What job does each choice do?
- What can I safely change?
- What has actually been verified?
- What still needs a human eye or target-application test?
