# Marks are not source code

The MIT License covers the open project, not the pitch.dog identity.

**pitch.dog**, **Letters & Light**, the pitch.dog logomark and related trade dress remain the property of pitch.dog. Forks may describe where the project came from, but may not suggest that pitch.dog made, reviewed or endorses the fork.

The clean route for a public fork:

1. keep the license and historical attribution;
2. replace the pitch.dog mark;
3. choose a distinct product name and visual identity;
4. say plainly that the fork is unofficial.

Questions about good-faith use can be opened as a repository discussion or issue.
